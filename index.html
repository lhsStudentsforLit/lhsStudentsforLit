  • About

    Lynbrook’s Students For Literacy (SFL) club aims to promote the love of reading within the Lynbrook school community by organizing interesting club meetings (i.e. book discussions, poetry readings, and creative writing) and book matching events in which students receive a book based on their responses to an interest survey. The club also spreads the importance of reading outside the school community through volunteer reading sessions and writing workshops. The goal of this club is to encourage people with any socioeconomic status to empower themselves through reading.

  • Our Mission:

    To promote the love of reading both within and outside of our school community.

Activities Overview

  • Because the goal of Lynbrook’s Students for Literacy club is to promote the love of reading within the school community and beyond, the club has two sets of activities: school activities and outside activities.
  • School activities take place on Lynbrook campus, either during the weekly meetings or other school related events.
  • Outside activities include most of the community outreach and volunteering programs.

Book Matching

During the school year, Lynbrook’s Students For Literacy club organizes 3-4 book matching events throughout the school campus.

Through this event, club members match each participant with a “perfect” book based on their response to an interest survey. The interest survey asks questions including: “what were the last three books you read and enjoyed?” or “what genre would you prefer?”.

A book and a personal note will be sent to each participant before a school break. From there, the participants who filled out a book review would be invited to a post celebration.

Book Drives

One important aspect to promote the love of reading is to provide the means to read.

Lynbrook’s Students for Literacy club aims to organize a book drive on campus in order to collect well-loved books from Lynbrook students and donate them to people who need them the most.

​Literally, we are spreading literacy.

Book Talks

Lynbrook Students for Literacy will be hosting a book talk during the beginning of every month in order to share new book arrivals and provide book recommendations. SFL's club adviser, Ms. Ashworth will be spokesperson for the book talks.

Writing Workshop Overview

In order to understand the literature one reads, one must be able to fit into the writer’s shoes.

Lynbrook Students for Literacy club organizes monthly writing workshops to teach young children (elementary school and middle school) creative writing and expository writing.

@Calabazas Library, San Jose

On every third Thursday of the month from 4 to 5 pm, members from the SFL club will go to the community center located in the Calabazas library and teach elementary school and middle school students the art of creative writing.
